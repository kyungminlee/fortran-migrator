# Routine cross-reference: Netlib → migrated targets

Per-routine name lookup across the upstream Netlib (single + double)
and the three migrator targets (kind10 / kind16 / multifloats).
Each row is a *stem* — the routine identity stripped of its
precision/family prefix; columns show the prefixed name as it
appears in source / object files. `—` means that target does not
expose this stem (the migrator did not emit it, or the upstream
has no counterpart in that precision).

Generated by `tools/gen_procedures.py` from staged source trees at
`/tmp/stg-{kind10,kind16,multifloats}/<lib>/src/` and the Netlib
references at `external/lapack-3.12.1/{BLAS,LAPACK}/SRC` and
`external/scalapack-2.2.3/{PBLAS,SRC}`.

**Prefix map**

| family | single | double | kind10 | kind16 | multifloats |
|--------|--------|--------|--------|--------|-------------|
| real (R) | s | d | e | q | m |
| complex (C) | c | z | y | x | w |
| real-from-complex (RC) | sc | dz | ey | qx | mw |
| complex-with-real-scalar (CR) | cs | zd | ye | xq | wm |
| int-of-real (IR) | is | id | ie | iq | im |
| int-of-complex (IC) | ic | iz | iy | ix | iw |

For PBLAS and ScaLAPACK every name additionally carries a
leading `p` (e.g. `pdgemm` / `pqgemm` / `pmgemm`). ScaLAPACK
also has a small `b`-prefix family for back-transformation
helpers (`bdlaapp`, `bdlaexc`, `bdtrexc`); their cells
therefore start with `b` rather than `p`.

Out of scope for this index: PBLAS-internal helper libraries
(`ptzblas`, `pbblas`) and ScaLAPACK's C-side wrappers
(`scalapack_c`). These ship in their own staged subtrees.
